I conducted research on two international mental health–related issues: substance use of “kush” in Sierra Leone and cyberbullying and school violence in Ghana. For each topic, I identified scholarly and policy-based sources outlining the scope of the problem and examined innovative interventions and programs designed to address these challenges. 

I also developed a capability statement for the Youth and Mental Health Resilience Program in the United States. This involved synthesizing language from previous organizational statements, integrating relevant data on youth mental health trends in the U.S., and articulating the program’s strategic importance. The goal was to produce a cohesive and compelling document that clearly communicated Project HOPE’s capacity and impact in youth mental health programming.

A key skill I developed was learning how to draft a capability statement, a professional writing format that was new to me. The main challenge was determining how to structure the document effectively while maintaining high-quality, persuasive language. I addressed this by using previous statements as structural guides and adapting them to fit updated research and program priorities.

I aim to begin drafting original written pieces focused on youth mental health and the implementation of Project HOPE’s programs. I am looking forward to transitioning from research synthesis to producing independent written analysis.
