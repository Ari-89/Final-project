I focused on meeting staff across departments, understanding organizational structures, and aligning my internship goals with ongoing projects. As an international student, my initial off-campus placement was adjusted to an on-campus internship. Although summer is a quieter period, I identified meaningful opportunities to contribute, particularly in program evaluation.

I decided to center my internship around program evaluation and integrate this focus across all projects. My responsibilities included creating and managing a partner organization database, developing a program evaluation framework, drafting content ideas for a 15th-year anniversary and annual report, completing background check procedures for a civic engagement program, and contributing to the development of a questionnaire for website rebranding.

A key insight I gained relates to cross-cultural communication styles. Coming from a context where indirect communication and consensus-building are common, I observed that a more direct and assertive approach is encouraged in my current environment. Navigating this shift required intentional effort. I made a conscious decision to communicate my interests and goals more openly and to articulate how I could contribute to team projects. Preparing to work at a middle school in Ward 7 also prompted deeper reflection on community context, local challenges, and how evaluation frameworks can incorporate anthropological perspectives.

I plan to continue updating the partner database, refine the program evaluation framework, conduct a site visit to observe and take field notes, and finalize and distribute the website rebranding questionnaire. I will also facilitate brainstorming sessions with team members.
